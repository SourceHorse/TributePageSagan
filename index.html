Carl Sagan

Astronomer - Author - Science Communicator

Some of Carl Sagan's contributions to science and society:

NASA

  • Worked on the Mariner 2 team exploring Venusian atmosphere and surface temperatures
  • Briefed astronauts of the Apollo program before their missions to the moon
  • Designed the golden plaques on the Pioneer 10 and 11 space probes
  • Worked on the Voyager Golden Record sent out of the solar system on Voyagers 1 and 2
  • Convinced NASA to turn Voyager 1 around to take the iconic Pale Blue Dot photograph
Cornell
  • Served as Director of the Laboratory for Planetary Studies
  • Became the David Duncan Professor of Astronomy and Space Sciences
  • Was the Associate Director of the Center for Radiophysics and Space Research
Science, Education, and Culture
  • Co-wrote and hosted the documentary series, Cosmos: A Personal Voyage
  • Wrote the science fiction novel, Contact, Which became a film
  • Was a very prominent advocate for the Search of Extraterrestrial Intelligence (SETI)
  • Strongly pushed for nuclear disarmament during the Cold War
  • Wrote many nonfiction works concerning the universe and humanity's place in it
  • Was an advocate for scientific litteracy and critical thinking
